WRITING STYLE REQUIREMENTS FOR ACADEMIC PAPER

Write in a professional, academic, ACM-style format with the following strict requirements:

TONE & STYLE:
- Professional and academic
- Human-written, NOT AI-generated
- Natural and conversational academic tone
- Avoid mechanical or formulaic patterns
- Vary sentence structure and length
- Sound like a researcher analyzing their work

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Keep sentences short and concise
- Avoid overly complex constructions
- Use simple, direct language when possible
- Break long sentences into shorter ones
- One idea per sentence

PUNCTUATION & FORMATTING:
- NO em-dashes (â€”)
- NO emojis
- Use commas, periods, and semicolons appropriately
- Use parentheses for asides and lists
- Standard academic punctuation only

CONTENT REQUIREMENTS:
- Short and concise overall
- Every word must serve a purpose
- Remove redundancy
- Focus on key findings and methods
- No unnecessary elaboration

LANGUAGE PATTERNS TO AVOID (AI-written indicators):
- "This demonstrates that..."
- "It is important to note that..."
- "Moreover," "Furthermore," "Additionally" at start of every sentence
- Repetitive sentence starters
- Overly formal or stilted language
- Lists of factors without context
- Mechanical transitions

LANGUAGE PATTERNS TO USE (Human-written indicators):
- Varied sentence starters
- Natural flow between ideas
- Direct statements without hedging (when appropriate)
- Active voice where suitable
- Specific examples over generalizations
- Results-focused language
- Concise technical descriptions

TECHNICAL WRITING:
- Define metrics clearly once, then use them
- Present results directly
- Compare meaningfully
- Interpret findings, don't just state them
- Acknowledge limitations honestly
- Use standard academic vocabulary

ORGANIZATION:
- Clear subsections
- Logical flow
- Each paragraph has one main point
- Smooth transitions between paragraphs
- Results before interpretation

REMEMBER: Write like a human researcher would write, not like an AI assistant would generate.
